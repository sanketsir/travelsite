Sinhgad Fort

Sinhagad is an ancient hill fortress located at around 49 km southwest of the city of Pune, India. Previously known as Kondhana, the fort had been the site of many battles, most notably the Battle of Sinhagad in 1670. It is Located near the Pune district in Maharashtra.

Dagadusheth Halwai Ganapati Temple

The Dagadusheth Halwai Ganapati temple is a Hindu Temple located in Pune and is dedicated to the Hindu god Ganesh. The temple is visited by over hundred thousand pilgrims every year.

Shaniwar Wada

Shaniwar Wada is a historical fortification in the city of Pune, India.Built in 1732,it was the great seat of the Peshwas of the Maratha Empire until 1818. Following the rise of the Maratha Empire, the palace became the center of Indian politics.

Raja Dinkar Kelkar Museum

The Raja Dinkar Kelkar Museum is in Pune, Maharashtra, India. It contains the collection of Dr. Dinkar G. Kelkar (1896-1990), dedicated to the memory of his only son, Raja. The three-storey building houses various sculptures dating back to the 14th century..

Adventures

Pune is a city that offers a variety of exciting adventures for those seeking a thrill. For starters, you can take a trek to the top of Sinhagad Fort, which offers breathtaking views of the city and the surrounding hills. Another popular activity is white water rafting in the Kundalika River, where you can experience the rush of the rapids and the beauty of the surrounding dense forests and historical places.

Route

Pune has a variety of routes for travelling that offer scenic views and memorable experiences. One popular route is the Mumbai-Pune Expressway, which is a six-lane highway that connects Mumbai and Pune. This route is known for its smooth ride and stunning views of the Western Ghats. Another popular route is the Mulshi Dam Road,dense forests,and breathtaking waterfalls.

Experince

Travelling in Pune is an experience that can be both thrilling and fulfilling. The city offers a unique blend of modernity and tradition, where you can explore ancient monuments, vibrant markets, and trendy cafes, all in one day. The local cuisine is a must-try, with dishes like Vada Pav, Misal Pav, and Pithla Bhakri, which are sure to tantalize your taste buds,every moment spent travelling in Pune is a new adventure.
